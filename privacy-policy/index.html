Privacy Policy

Your privacy matters. This page explains what data is involved when you use dev-codelist.net and how it is handled.

1 Overview

dev-codelist.net is a static website. We do not operate user accounts, store login credentials, or run our own backend servers. This means the amount of personal data we directly collect and process is minimal by design. This policy describes what third-party services are used and what data may be processed as a result of your visit.

2 Data We Do Not Collect

We do not collect, store, or process any of the following on our end:

  • › Names, email addresses, or contact details (unless you voluntarily contact us)
  • › Account credentials or passwords
  • › Payment information
  • › Tracking cookies set by us

3 Hosting & Server Logs

This website is hosted on GitHub Pages. When you visit, GitHub's servers may automatically record standard server log data such as your IP address, browser type, referring URL, and the pages you visit. This data is handled by GitHub under their own privacy policy. We do not have direct access to these logs. For details, please refer to the GitHub Privacy Statement.

4 Third-Party Services

To provide a functional and visually consistent experience, this site loads resources from the following third-party services. Each may process your IP address or other technical identifiers as part of serving the resource:

Google Fonts

Used to load the Plus Jakarta Sans and JetBrains Mono typefaces. Google may log your IP address when the font files are fetched. See the Google Privacy Policy.

Tailwind CSS CDN

The Tailwind CSS stylesheet is loaded from a public CDN. Your IP address may be logged by the CDN provider as part of a standard request. No personal profiling is performed.

5 Cookies

This website does not set any first-party cookies. Third-party services listed above may set their own technical cookies or use browser storage as part of their normal operation. These are not under our control. You can manage or block cookies through your browser settings at any time.

6 Contact & Voluntary Data

If you use the Contact page to reach us, the form opens your local mail application with a pre-filled message. No data is transmitted to or stored by our servers through this form — communication happens directly through your own email provider.

7 Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights under applicable data protection laws (such as the GDPR) including the right to access, rectify, or erase personal data held about you, and the right to object to or restrict its processing. Since we do not store personal data directly, most requests should be directed to the relevant third-party providers. For any other privacy-related inquiry, please contact us via the Contact page.

8 Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy if our practices change or to reflect changes in applicable law. Continued use of the site after any update means you accept the revised policy. We will make reasonable efforts to highlight significant changes.
